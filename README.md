CSAT Analysis Dashboard
Overview

This project analyzes Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) data to understand customer experience, support performance, and operational efficiency. Using SQL for data preparation and Power BI for visualization, the dashboard provides insights into customer sentiment, service quality, SLA performance, and call trends.

Objectives
Measure overall customer satisfaction.
Analyze customer sentiment distribution.
Identify major reasons for customer support interactions.
Evaluate SLA compliance.
Compare performance across communication channels.
Discover geographical trends in customer interactions.
Tools & Technologies
SQL – Data cleaning and analysis
Power BI – Dashboard creation and visualization
Excel/CSV – Dataset source
Key KPIs
Average CSAT Score
Total Customer Interactions
SLA Compliance Rate
Customer Sentiment Distribution
Channel-wise Performance
State-wise Call Volume
Key Insights
Average CSAT score is 2.07/10, indicating low customer satisfaction.
Over 62.7% of interactions received a CSAT score of 0.
Around 52% of customers expressed negative sentiment.
Billing-related issues account for approximately 70% of customer interactions.
All communication channels show consistently low satisfaction scores.
Approximately 37% of interactions failed to meet SLA targets.
California, Texas, and Florida generated the highest number of customer interactions.
Dashboard Features
KPI Summary Cards
CSAT Score Analysis
Sentiment Distribution
Call Reason Analysis
SLA Performance Tracking
Channel-wise Comparison
Geographic Analysis
Business Recommendations
Improve billing clarity and issue resolution processes.
Reduce response times to improve SLA compliance.
Focus on customer retention strategies for dissatisfied customers.
Prioritize improvements in high-volume states.
Enhance service quality across all support channels.
Project Outcome

The dashboard helps stakeholders monitor customer satisfaction, identify service bottlenecks, and make data-driven decisions to improve overall customer experience.
